🚀 Daily workflow — 5 steps
  1. Open the Entry tab — a new blank form is ready.
  2. Fill the 9 sections top-to-bottom. Tap "Mark as Complete" inside each one.

🔧 How clearances are measured
Rudder Clearance

Feeler gauges inserted between the rudder stock (or pintle) and its bushing at four positions: Forward, Aft, Port, Starboard. The thickness of the largest blade that fits is the clearance. Values in mm.

📐 See illustration →
Propeller Shaft Clearance

Feeler gauges at Top, Bottom, A, B positions for each bearing (Outboard, Inboard, and Strut where applicable). Values in mm.

BP / AP: "Before Pulling" = initial reading; "After Pulling" = after the shaft was removed for servicing. If not pulled, BP equals AP.

📐 See illustration →
Tailshaft & Propeller — Crack Test (NDT)

Non-Destructive Testing using dye penetrant (or magnetic particle). Surface is cleaned, red dye is applied and left to seep into any cracks, then developer pulls the dye out so cracks appear as red lines. Pass / Fail / N/A recorded per zone.

📐 See illustration →
Chain Calibration

Calipers measure the thickness of each link bar at two perpendicular axes (X = horizontal, Y = vertical). 7 shackles × 4 links × 2 axes × 2 sides = 112 measurements. The app calculates the mean automatically — you only enter raw readings.

📐 See illustration →

👷 All clearance measurements come from the Propulsion Personnel-in-Charge. Chain measurements come from Hull/Deck team. Get their measurement worksheets during drydock inspection.

☁️ Cloud drafts: two-device workflow
  1. Phone A: fill in some data → tap "☁️ Push". Draft appears in cloud, marked 🔓 Yours.
  2. Phone B: Drafts tab → ☁️ Cloud Drafts → ↻ Refresh → tap "Pull & Edit".
  3. Phone B edits. Phone A sees the draft as 🔒 Locked.
  4. Phone B: tap ☁️ Push to save and release lock, or Mark Complete to finalize.

💡 Lock auto-expires after 2 hours.
